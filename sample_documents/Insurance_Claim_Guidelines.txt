Insurance Claim Guidelines

Version: 2026

General Guidelines:

- Always inform the insurance company immediately after hospitalization or an insured event.
- Keep all original bills and supporting documents.
- Submit claims only through the official claim portal or authorized representatives.
- Claims with incomplete documentation may be rejected.

Required Documents:

For Health Insurance Claims:
- Hospital discharge summary.
- Doctor's prescription.
- Original medical bills and receipts.
- Diagnostic reports.
- Government-issued ID proof.

For Travel Insurance Claims:
- Passport copy.
- Flight tickets.
- Police report (if baggage or passport is lost).
- Medical reports (if applicable).
- Claim application form.

Claim Timelines:

- Health insurance reimbursement claims must be submitted within 30 days.
- Travel insurance claims must be submitted within 15 days.
- Emergency claims should be reported within 24 hours whenever possible.

Cashless Claims:

- Cashless hospitalization is available only at network hospitals.
- Pre-authorization documents may be required before admission.

Common Reasons for Claim Rejection:

- Non-disclosure of pre-existing diseases.
- Submission of forged or altered documents.
- Claims raised after the allowed time period.
- Treatment for excluded medical conditions.
- Failure to provide mandatory supporting documents.

Frequently Asked Questions:

Q. What happens if I miss the claim submission deadline?
A. The insurance company may reject the claim unless exceptional circumstances are accepted.

Q. Can I submit scanned copies of documents?
A. Yes, if permitted by the insurer's online claim portal.

Q. Are all medical expenses reimbursed?
A. No, only covered expenses mentioned in the policy documents are reimbursed.
